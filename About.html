About Me

My journey into tech began not in a classroom, but with a secondhand computer that barely worked. It ran hot, crashed constantly, and forced me to learn how computers operate from the inside out. That broken machine lit a fire in me — a curiosity about technology that never faded. Over the years, that curiosity sharpened into a passion for cybersecurity, data science, and building software that solves meaningful problems.

Today, I'm a Computer Science major at Harvey Mudd College, where I study systems, software, and security with intensity and heart. I've had the opportunity to lead high-impact research, build machine learning models, develop brain-computer interface systems, and serve in multiple leadership roles — including Dorm President, CS Mentorship Director, and founder of student organizations. I'm currently an AI Researcher at the Harvey Mudd College APE Lab, where I study AI and it's applications in environmental science and cybersecurity — a perfect blend of my passions for defense and innovation.

I'm the kind of builder who thrives at the intersection of mission and engineering. Whether it's deploying secure infrastructure, visualizing intelligence data, or mentoring future technologists, I care deeply about both the code and the cause. If my work can support national defense, uplift communities, or protect information — then I'm on the right path.

Outside of tech, I find peace in faith, intensity in lifting, and joy in racing games and cooking. My heritage is Indian and I'm a first-generation American, driven to succeed not for the resume, but for my family and future.

Feel free to check out my contact section to reach out, especially if you're building in defense, security, or just want to connect on cool problems.
